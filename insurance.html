Insurance Companies

Wildlife-vehicle collisions cost U.S. insurers over $1 billion annually in comprehensive claims. AWCMP reduces the frequency of those events and delivers the validated corridor data actuaries need to price risk accurately.

🏛️
INSURANCE
Auto · Commercial · Life · Health · Reinsurance
Risk Reduction Partner

Wildlife-vehicle collisions are among the most underpriced risks in rural auto insurance — not because insurers don't care, but because the data doesn't exist to price them accurately. Claims arrive without corridor context, without verified wildlife involvement, and without any signal about whether a given road segment is trending worse or better.

AWCMP changes the data landscape entirely. For the first time, insurers gain access to verified corridor-level collision frequency data — real-time and historical — that supports actuarial modeling, risk rating refinement, and loss prevention program development in rural markets.

And beyond data: AWCMP directly reduces the events that generate claims. Fewer deer-vehicle strikes. Fewer unwitnessed crashes that become total losses because no one called EMS in time.

Actuarial-Grade Corridor Risk Data

Verified, timestamped collision event data by corridor segment — enabling more accurate risk rating for rural auto policies, commercial fleet coverage, and agricultural vehicle insurance in wildlife-dense regions.

Reduced Severity Through Faster EMS

Automatic crash-to-EMS notification reduces the time between roadway departure and medical response — directly reducing the injury severity and medical cost that drives bodily injury claim values.

Validated Incident Documentation

Sensor-verified timestamps and event data for wildlife strikes and roadway departures — supporting claims investigation, fraud reduction, and dispute resolution in rural incident claims.

AWCMP corridor data identifies the specific road segments generating disproportionate collision claims — enabling targeted loss control programs, customer advisories, and underwriting adjustments by location.

Loss Prevention Program Integration
